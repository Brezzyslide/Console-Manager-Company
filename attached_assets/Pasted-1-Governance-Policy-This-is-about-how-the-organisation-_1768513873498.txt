1. Governance & Policy

This is about how the organisation is run, controlled, and held accountable.

Core governance documents

Governance framework or governance charter

Organisational structure chart

Delegations of authority

Roles and responsibilities matrix

Fit and Proper Person declarations (directors, key management)

Conflict of interest register

Board or management meeting minutes (or equivalent governance records)

Policy suite (current, approved, reviewed)

Incident management policy

Complaints management policy

Risk management policy

Continuous improvement policy

Feedback and feedback handling policy

Privacy and confidentiality policy

Information management and record keeping policy

Code of conduct

Child safe standards policy (where applicable)

Whistleblower or reportable conduct policy (if applicable)

Quality and risk

Risk register (organisation level)

Continuous improvement register / quality improvement plan

Internal audit reports

Previous external audit reports and responses

Management review records

Governance evidence of implementation

Policy register showing review dates

Evidence policies are communicated to staff

Training or induction records referencing governance policies

2. Staff & Personnel Compliance

This is about who is allowed to deliver care, and whether they are safe, qualified, and supervised.

Pre-employment and screening

Police check records

Working With Children Check (where required)

NDIS Worker Screening clearance

Right to work documentation

Reference checks (where applicable)

Qualifications and role suitability

Qualification certificates relevant to role

Scope of practice or role description

Evidence qualifications are appropriate to supports delivered

Training and competency

Training matrix / training register

Mandatory training records (e.g. incident management, medication, restrictive practices)

Induction records

Ongoing professional development records

Supervision and performance

Supervision schedules

Supervision session records

Performance reviews or appraisals

Corrective action or performance improvement plans (if applicable)

Workforce governance

Staff register (roles, employment type, start dates)

Contractor agreements (if contractors used)

Rostering policies relating to skill mix and ratios

3. Operational / Service Delivery (Actual delivery of care)

This is where auditors look for evidence that supports are delivered as agreed and funded.

Participant documentation

Service agreements

Participant consent forms

Care plans / support plans

Behaviour support plans (if applicable)

Mealtime management plans (if applicable)

Risk assessments specific to the participant

Delivery evidence

Rosters and shift allocations

Timesheets or shift completion records

Case notes / progress notes

Daily logs or support records

Activity participation records

Clinical and support practice

Medication management plans

Medication administration records (MARs)

Incident reports linked to participants

Restrictive practice records and reporting evidence (where applicable)

Funding and claiming

Service bookings or funding approvals

Invoices or claims evidence

Reconciliation between roster, delivery, and claiming

Evidence rates align with pricing arrangements

4. Site-Specific & Environment (Place based evidence)

This is about whether the environment itself is safe and suitable for care.

Site safety and readiness

Site risk assessments

Emergency and evacuation plans (site specific)

Fire safety plans

Emergency drills or evacuation records

Maintenance logs

Environment controls

Infection control procedures and cleaning schedules

Food safety records (if meals provided)

Equipment maintenance records

Asset registers for assistive equipment

Local implementation evidence

Site induction records for staff

Staff access instructions (keys, alarms, exits)

Incident logs linked to the site

Visitor or contractor sign in records (if relevant)

5. Cross-cutting documents auditors often ask for everywhere

These appear across all domains and often expose weaknesses:

Document register with version control

Evidence documents are in date and reviewed

Evidence staff can access key documents

Records showing policies are actually used

Alignment between documents and practice
