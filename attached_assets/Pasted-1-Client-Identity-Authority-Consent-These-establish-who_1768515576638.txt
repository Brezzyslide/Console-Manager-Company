1. Client Identity, Authority & Consent

These establish who the person is and who can decide.

Client profile / intake record

NDIS plan (current and previous if relevant)

NDIS number and plan dates

Service agreement (signed, current)

Consent forms:

Consent to provide supports

Consent to share information

Consent to medication (if applicable)

Guardian / nominee documentation (if applicable)

Evidence of supported decision making (where relevant)

Auditors look for: alignment between authority, consent, and services delivered.

2. Assessment & Planning Records

These show why supports exist and how they’re meant to work.

Comprehensive care / support plan

Goals and outcomes linked to the NDIS plan

Risk assessments (client-specific)

Functional assessments (if used)

Behaviour support plan (if applicable)

Mealtime management plan (if applicable)

Communication plan

Health management plan

Disaster / emergency response section within the care plan

Auditors look for: currency, personalisation, funding alignment, and clarity.

3. Delivery of Supports (Proof of Care)

This is the evidence that care actually happened.

Rosters showing client allocation

Shift notes / case notes

Daily support logs

Activity participation records

Progress notes against goals

Community access records

Overnight / supervision logs (where relevant)

Auditors look for: consistency, accuracy, and alignment with the care plan.

4. Medication & Health Records (if applicable)

These are high-risk and closely scrutinised.

Medication management plan

Medication administration records (MARs)

PRN protocols and usage logs

Medication reviews

Incident reports related to medication

Evidence of staff competency for medication support

Auditors look for: accuracy, timeliness, and safe practice.

5. Incidents, Complaints & Safeguards

This shows how risk is managed around the individual.

Incident reports involving the client

Incident follow-up actions

Behavioural incidents and de-escalation records

Restrictive practice records and authorisations (if applicable)

Complaints made by or on behalf of the client

Outcomes and resolution records

Auditors look for: responsiveness, learning, and safeguards.

6. Funding, Claims & Financial Alignment

This proves supports match what’s funded.

Service bookings or funding allocation records

Invoices or claim summaries for the client

Evidence rostered hours align with claims

Variations or changes to supports

Evidence client was informed of changes

Auditors look for: transparency and accuracy, not accounting perfection.

7. Reviews, Monitoring & Change Management

This shows the service is not static.

Care plan review records

Progress reviews against goals

Changes to support needs and rationale

Evidence of client or representative involvement in reviews

Transition or exit planning (if applicable)

Auditors look for: responsiveness and continuous adjustment.

8. Client Safety & Environment (where site-based)

These are client-specific even though they involve a site.

Client-specific emergency or evacuation needs

Room or environment risk assessments (SIL/SDA)

Behavioural or safety considerations related to the environment

Evidence staff are aware of site-specific risks for that client

Auditors look for: individualised safety, not generic plans.

9. Cross-cutting client file checks (what auditors always test)

These aren’t documents, but patterns auditors check across the file:

Records are in date and consistent

Names, dates, and details match across documents

Care plan aligns with daily records

Incidents align with case notes

Funding aligns with delivery

Client voice is visible in planning and reviews
